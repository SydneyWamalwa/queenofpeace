Socio-Economic and Environment Programme

For generations, pastoral women in northern Kenya have had limited access to resources due to a number of factors, including armed conflict, abject poverty, and traditions. They have also had to rely on the ecologically-devastating charcoal burning for income.

Our Socio-Economic and Environment Programme Aims to Tackle These Issues Through:

  • Pursuit of socio-economic alternatives that protect our environment and positive culture.
  • Pursuit of socio-economic alternatives that improve our health and nutrition, such as:
    • Establishment of kitchen gardens to improve nutrition and food security.
    • Awareness creation of the effects of retrogressive cultural practices, such as early girls child marriages and female genital mutilation (FGM).
    • Tree planting of indigenous trees and fruit trees.
    • Exploring cooking alternatives, such as the use of energy-saving jikos.
    • Exploring income-generating activities that promote and preserve positive culture, including our artifacts and music.
